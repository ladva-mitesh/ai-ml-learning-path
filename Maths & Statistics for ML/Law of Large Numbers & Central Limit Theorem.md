# 📘 Law of Large Numbers (LLN) & Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

---

# 1. Law of Large Numbers (LLN)

## Definition

The **Law of Large Numbers (LLN)** states:

> As the **sample size increases**, the **sample average** gets closer to the **true population average (expected value).**

### Example

**Coin Toss**

* 10 Tosses → 8 Heads (80%)
* 100 Tosses → 53 Heads (53%)
* 10,000 Tosses → 5007 Heads (50.07%)

As the number of tosses increases, the result approaches the true probability (50%).

### Key Points

* More observations → Better estimate.
* Sample mean converges to the population mean.
* Improves the accuracy of predictions and estimates.

**Memory Trick:**
**More Data → Better Average**

---

# 2. Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

## Definition

The **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)** states:

> If we repeatedly take **large random samples** from any population, the **distribution of the sample means** becomes approximately **Normal (Bell-shaped)**, regardless of the original population's distribution.

### Example

Suppose the population of incomes is highly skewed.

* Take a sample of 30 people.
* Calculate the average income.
* Repeat this process 1000 times.

The graph of all sample averages forms a **Normal Distribution (Bell Curve)**.

### Key Points

* Original population can have any shape.
* Sample means become approximately Normally distributed.
* Foundation of confidence intervals and hypothesis testing.

**Memory Trick:**
**Many Sample Averages → Bell Curve**

---

# LLN vs CLT

| Law of Large Numbers (LLN)                  | Central Limit Theorem (CLT)                 |
| ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Focuses on one large sample.                | Focuses on many large samples.              |
| Sample mean approaches the population mean. | Sample means form a Normal Distribution.    |
| Improves estimation accuracy.               | Describes the distribution of sample means. |

---

# Quick Revision

### Law of Large Numbers (LLN)

* Large sample → Accurate average.
* Sample mean ≈ Population mean.
* **Focus:** Accuracy.

### Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

* Many large samples.
* Distribution of sample means → Bell Curve.
* **Focus:** Distribution of sample means.
